PENSTRAAT - Past / Present / Future

The Penstraat originated in the 18th century as a suburb of the first settlement Punda, which had already started to become densely populated. The Penstraat, once the area of the Curacao well-to-do, has undertaken a huge make-over after a long period of neglect and abatement. The Penstraat can look back on a vibrant past and has been writing history once again in recent years. Thanks to serious renovations over the last 10 years, the area’s decay has been halted and actually turned around.

The Penstraat is located just off the lively center of Willemstad, never more than a stones throw away from the ocean. (literally!) Perhaps best known for its historic cultural value on UNESCO’s ‘World heritage list’ - nowadays a popular neighborhood on the island for a number of reasons. Explore the island's rich history, architecture, night life, culinary highlights and enjoy some of the best oceanside accommodations. Where can you find this eclectic mix of Caribbean culture and entertainment? The Penstraat!

Get to know the district where history and future meets and be a witness of the renovation and revival of this historical and cultural masterpiece! Not all neglected buildings are restored yet, but that is what gives the neighbourhood that special vivid and raw edge. While strolling through the area during the daytime, you will sometimes feel like a time traveler. At night it becomes a foodie heaven for all tastes, ages and budgets.

Octagon Museum

Witness a piece of world history

The Octagon Museum houses the Bolívar exhibit in memory of the Curaçao connection to Simón Bolívar (1783-1830). In 1812 after the first stage of the revolt in Venezuela had failed, Bolívar took refuge on the island of Curaçao.
